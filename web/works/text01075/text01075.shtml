Childe Harold
 
Stout and stark, a sable bark
 
Sails in sorrow on the tide.
 
There black-suited men sit muted
 
As a deathwatch at his side.
5
Dead he’s lying, death-defying
 
Poet’s face now marble-white;
 
His blue eyes now pierce the skies,
 
Still upturned to heaven’s light.
 
Waves are flailing as if wailing
10
From a nixie bride’s deep sighs,
 
And the surges break like dirges
 
On the bark with mournful cries.
Translation:Draper, Hal
