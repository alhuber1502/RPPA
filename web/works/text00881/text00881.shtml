On the Death of His Brother Giovanni
[Sonnet 10]
 
One day, if I am not forever fleeing
 
From people to people, you will see me sitting
 
On your stone, O my brother, shedding tears
 
For the fallen flower of your gentle years.
5
Mother, drawing out her late day now, alone,
 
Talks about me to your silent ashes:
 
While to both of you I stretch deluded hands
 
And alone from afar I salute my home.
 
I feel the hostile gods, and the secret grief
10
That stirred up such a tempest in your life,
 
While in your harbor I too pray for rest.
 
Of so many hopes now only this remains:
 
O Strangers, at least give back my bones
 
To my poor mother's grieving breast.
Translation:Ferber, Michael
