Pleasure in Death
 
Before he sinks into the dark blue stream
 
The death-drunk swan sings in his dream;
 
Earth, summer-weary, as its flowers go,
 
Leaves all its fire to grapes to make them glow;
5
Sun, emitting flashes as it sinks,
 
Once more gives out its glow which earth now drinks,
 
Until, to clasp the drunk one near and far,
 
Wondrous night arises star by star.
Translation:Ferber, Michael
