Rima I
 
I know a strange, gigantic hymn
 
in the soul’s night, that heralds the coming of day,
 
and these pages are part of that hymn,
 
a lingering cadence afloat in the shadowy air.
5
I wish I could write it down, and tame
 
man’s rebellious, imperfect tongue
 
into words that were laughter and sighs,
 
color and song, all in one.
 
But I struggle in vain for lack of ways
10
to catch it — oh, my beauty! — none comes near,
 
though when alone with you, I hold your hand in mine,
 
then might I almost sing it to your ear.
Translation:Popkin, Louise; Berg, Mary G.
