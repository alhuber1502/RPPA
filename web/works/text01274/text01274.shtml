André Chénier
To N. N. Raevsky
Ainsi, triste et captif, ma
lyre toutefois s’éveillait
 
While all the world, struck dumb with awe,
 
At Byron’s urn have come to gaze,
 
While he from choirs of Europe’s lyres
 
Next to Dante hears his praise,
5
To me another shade calls out
 
Who had no songs, no tears of grief,
 
When from the scaffold’s pain and blood
 
In his grave he found relief.
 
He sang of love and peaceful groves.
10
I bring him flowers overdue.
 
An unknown lyre now resounds:
 
My song is heard by him and you.
Translation:Ferber, Michael
