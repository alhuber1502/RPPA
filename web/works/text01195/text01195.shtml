Here, in so poor a tomb ...
 
Here, in so poor a tomb, are still the bones
 
of the sublime, one epic poet found,
 
who made the ancient trumpet highly sound
 
from pole to distant pole in modem tones?
5
And Rome, ah, Rome no worthy tombstone owns
 
for one whose flight made every sky rebound!
 
Is, then, her major shrine a dusty mound
 
of bishop-kings, a catacomb of moans?
 
Throng of dead men who never were alive,
10
depart at once, and let this Vatican,
 
cleansed of your stench, still rise and still survive!
 
There, at the center, let this man be placed,
 
where, worthy of them both, now soars the dome
 
which for great Tasso Buonarroti raised.
Translation:Tusiani, Joseph
