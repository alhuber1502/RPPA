At Dawn Tomorrow ...
 
At dawn tomorrow, when the land turns white,
 
Til leave, because I know you wait for me.
 
Til go through forests, up the mountain’s height.
 
I cannot stay away from you. I'll see
5
Nothing, my eyes fixed on my thoughts, alone,
 
And hear nothing, not a single sound or sight.
 
With my back bent, hands crossed, Til walk, unknown,
 
Sad, and day for me will be like night.
 
I will not see the golden end of day,
10
Nor sails descend to Harfleur far away.
 
When I arrive, Til put this on your tomb —
 
A bunch of holly sprigs, heather in bloom.
Translation:Goldberg, Midge
